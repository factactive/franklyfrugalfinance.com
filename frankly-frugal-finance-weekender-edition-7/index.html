Frankly Frugal Finance: Weekender Edition #7

Posted on August 23, 2019 by Kipp

Happy weekend everyone!  Summer weekends are just busy as usual.  Can’t work on the deck because of all the rain throughout the week, may not be quiet dry enough yet.  Other than that visiting family and just enjoying the weekend.  I did get a book in the mail Friday, Outsmarting the System, if you wonder what it is about I won it from a giveaway over at Debt Discipline.  So feel free to read the review over there, I am still working on finishing the library book so I am not sure when I will get around to it, but I plan on it soon(ish).

Anywho, I had another couple posts this week, they are:

August Stock Purchase and Dividend Update

Why you Should NOT Delay Parenthood

So if you haven’t read them yet, take a gander.  Well, I have a busy rest of the day, so I will be frank and get some of the articles i liked this week:

How Long Would Your Ideal Work Week Be

John @ Wisedollar put together an excellent post about our typical work week.  Be honest with your self, would you work as much as you do if you didn’t have to?  I think many people would like to work or volunteer in some fashion if / once financially independent.  But you probably wouldn’t be doing that for 40 hours or more a week unless if you absolutely loved it.  Food for thought!

Behind the Scenes of a Happy Frugal Marriage

Do you have a spouse of a significant other in which you need to talk to about financial goals?  Then this post from Mrs. Frugalwoods is made for you!  She goes in great detail as to how they come together as a team to make financial decisions and explains how you can as well.

5 Ways to Frugal Living

Tawcan has a nice post with some simple frugal ideas.  I would suggest checking it out, they are all simple, but they add up!  I have been doing all 5 of these before I started blogging, but some of these I have only recently started doing.  Saving money is half the battle!  The first half is earning it…

The Job Switch-A-Roo

Ok, so this is an interesting story-line from Bryan @ Income Surfer.  Basically he goes from laying himself off, to going to his first day at work where they seemed to not know what to do with him with his new boss off that day, to going in the next day to find out he has a different job than what he interviewed for.  What craziness!  You have to read it to get all the details.

Misplaced Faith

This one is actually a guest post of Kirsten’s at Indebted Mom over at Debt Debs.  Sometimes you need to have faith that everything will be ok to have the courage to make changes.  You can’t just say it… you have to believe it.  Do you have the faith that you can turn around your finances?  I bet you can.

Well, that is the wrap for this week.  Off to run some errands, have a great rest of the weekend everyone!
